# Gaming Industry Stock Market Analysis — Tableau

## Overview
This project analyzes stock market performance across the top 10 gaming companies from 2000 to 2024 using Tableau. The interactive dashboard tracks stock price trends, price volatility, trading volume, and market capitalization growth — offering a comprehensive view of how the gaming industry has evolved as an investment sector over the past two decades.

**🔗 [View Interactive Dashboard on Tableau Public](https://public.tableau.com/app/profile/aditi.aryal/viz/gamingdata_17332371263010/Dashboard1)**

---

## 🛠️ Tools & Technologies
- Tableau
- Excel / CSV (data preparation)

## 📂 Dataset
- **File:** `Top10VideoGameStocks.csv`
- **Coverage:** 2000–2024, monthly stock data
- **Companies:** Electronic Arts, Embracer Group, Microsoft Gaming, NetEase Games, Nintendo, Playtika, Roblox Corporation, Sony Interactive Entertainment, Take-Two Interactive, Tencent Interactive Entertainment
- **Key Fields:** Date, Company, Ticker Symbol, Open, High, Low, Close, Adjusted Close, Volume

## 📈 Visualizations & Key Findings

### 1️⃣ Gaming Industry Stock Performance Comparison (Line Chart)
Tracks the adjusted closing price of all 10 companies from 2000 to 2024.

**Key Findings:**
- Microsoft Gaming is the clear dominant performer, surging past $400 by 2024 — far outpacing all other companies
- Electronic Arts and Take-Two Interactive show steady long-term growth, reaching $100–$150 by the early 2020s
- Nintendo, NetEase, and Sony show moderate but consistent growth, reflecting their established market positions
- Newer entrants like Roblox and Playtika show relatively flat or declining trends, reflecting post-IPO corrections
- The 2020–2021 period shows a sharp spike across multiple companies, coinciding with pandemic-driven gaming demand

---

### 2️⃣ Stock Price Volatility and Trading Volume (Scatter Plot)
Plots price volatility against stock price (USD), with bubble size representing trading volume.

**Key Findings:**
- Microsoft Gaming shows the highest price levels alongside high volatility — its large bubbles indicate heavy trading volume at elevated prices
- Tencent Interactive shows high volatility with moderate price levels, clustered around $30–$80 with very large trading volumes
- Most companies cluster near the average volatility line, suggesting relatively stable long-term price behavior
- Companies with lower stock prices (Nintendo, Sony) show low volatility — consistent with mature, stable companies

---

### 3️⃣ Gaming Industry Market Capitalization Growth (Stacked Bar Chart)
Shows total market capitalization across all 10 companies by year, color-coded by company.

**Key Findings:**
- Combined market cap was minimal through the early 2000s, growing slowly through 2015
- The 2017–2021 period saw explosive growth, with total industry market cap reaching nearly $10,000 (indexed) at its 2021 peak
- Microsoft Gaming and Sony dominate the market cap composition in recent years, reflecting their acquisitions and platform dominance
- A slight pullback is visible in 2022–2023, consistent with broader tech market corrections
- The gaming industry's overall market cap trajectory reflects its transition from a niche entertainment sector to a mainstream investment category

---

## Conclusion
The gaming industry has transformed dramatically as an investment sector over the past two decades. Microsoft's aggressive acquisitions and platform strategy have made it the standout performer, while established players like EA, Take-Two, and Nintendo show steady, reliable growth. The 2020–2021 pandemic era created a significant but partially temporary spike across the sector. Volatility analysis confirms that higher stock prices tend to come with higher price swings, making gaming stocks attractive but risk-aware investments. Overall, the dashboard tells a story of an industry that has grown from a niche market into one of the most dynamic segments of the global stock market.
